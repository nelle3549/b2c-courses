Mini-Course 02

Problem First: Design Thinking for Founders

Course Intro · Most failed startups didn't fail at building.

A Manila team spends nine months building a beautiful app, then launches it to silence. After that, the one statistic that explains most startup deaths ("no market need"), and what the next ~40 minutes will train you to do so it never explains yours. Watch before Section 1.

Most failed startups didn't fail at building. They failed at choosing. This course teaches you to slow down before you ship: empathy interviews, behavioral observation, problem reframing, and how-might-we thinking, with the real moves of Kalibrr, GrowSari, First Circle, and PayMongo as your worked examples. As you go, you'll fill in a Problem Hypothesis Card for your own venture idea: the symptom, the questions, the underlying problem, the HMW, and a statement sharp enough to bet a weekend on. It compiles on its own page near the end: printable, and pointed at your first five interviews.

By the end, you'll be able to find and frame a problem worth solving, without burning months building something nobody needs.

  1. Understand Explain why “problem-first” beats “solution-first” using the CB Insights top-failure-reason data.
  2. Apply Conduct empathy interviews using behavioral, past-tense, specific prompts.
  3. Analyze Distinguish a symptom from an underlying problem using the 5 Whys and Jobs-to-be-Done lens.
  4. Evaluate Judge whether a problem statement is falsifiable within seven days.
