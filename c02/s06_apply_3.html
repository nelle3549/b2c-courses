Symptom or underlying problem?

Tap one answer per row.

"Nobody finishes our sign-up process."

Symptom: it describes what's visible in the funnel, not why. It's the top of an iceberg; the why lives below.

"Sign-up demands a TIN and two IDs before showing any value, and our users are unregistered micro-sellers."

Underlying problem: it names the structural mismatch between what the flow demands and who the users actually are. You can design against this.

"Customers keep saying it's too expensive."

Symptom: "too expensive" is usually a stand-in for "I don't see enough value to justify the cost." The underlying problem is which value driver is missing, not the peso figure.

"The product's value only shows up after three weeks of use, but the free trial ends in seven days."

Underlying problem: a structural gap between time-to-value and the trial window. Notice how it immediately suggests design directions; symptoms never do.
