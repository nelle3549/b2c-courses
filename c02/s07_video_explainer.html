Mid-lesson explainer

What an empathy interview actually sounds like.

The same question, asked two ways, and why only one of them returns real data.

You'll hear "Would you use an app that helps you find babysitters faster?", and the three ways that single sentence quietly ruins the answer. Then the empathy version of the same question, and the three follow-ups that pull the full story out of a user. This is Sections 2 and 3 turned into sound: the interview discipline, performed.

Watch for

  1. The three flaws named in the leading question (leading, hypothetical, solution-first) and the user's polite-but-empty reaction to it.
  2. The past-tense reframe ("Tell me about the last time…") and how the user's body language changes when the question lands on a real memory.
  3. The closing rule: behavior, not opinions · past, not future · specifics, not generalities. The same three gates your Field-2 questions must pass.
