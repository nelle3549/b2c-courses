Growing or scaling?

Ask the test question each time: what happens to costs when customers double?

A tutoring agency doubles its students by hiring one new tutor for every 15 enrollees.

Growing: the cost curve chases revenue. Every 15 students force a new hire, so doubling students roughly doubles payroll.

A review-app adds 10,000 new users in a month on the same servers and the same team of four.

Scaling: the engine was built once; 10,000 more users cost server pennies. Revenue can climb while the cost line stays nearly flat.

An online carinderia-ordering app expands, but each new neighborhood needs a new manual partnership, a redesigned menu, and on-site training.

Growing, despite the app. The next neighborhood costs the same lakad as the last one. Technology on top of a flat cost curve is a digitized small business, not a scaling startup.

A stock-learning platform serves its next 10,000 members with the same charts, lessons, and three founders.

Scaling: the Investagrams shape. Users multiplied year after year while the team stayed a handful; the product did the serving.
