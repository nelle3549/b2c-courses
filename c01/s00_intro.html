Mini-Course 01

Startup PH: Inside the Filipino Innovation Ecosystem

Course Intro · There's a startup ecosystem you didn't know existed.

Most students think "startup" means Silicon Valley: a hoodie, a pitch deck, funding in dollars. This opener dismantles that picture. There's a Philippine ecosystem running right now, with a law behind it (the Innovative Startup Act), government grant money, and incubators sitting inside universities you may already attend. Watch before Section 1.

"Startup" is the most misused word in Filipino business conversation. This course pins it down: the working definition, the legal one written into RA 11337, and the three marks that separate a startup from every other kind of venture (born as a project, built on a non-obvious solution, and able to scale instead of just grow). Then it hands you the map of the PH ecosystem and the doors already open to you, with the real moves of Coins.ph, Packworks, Investagrams, and Mayani as your worked examples. As you go, you'll fill in a Startup Reality Check for your own idea: what it is, what makes it non-obvious, whether it scales, and which door you'll walk through first. It compiles on its own page near the end: printable, and pointed at your first real move.

By the end, you'll know exactly what kind of venture you're holding, and which door in the PH ecosystem it opens.

  1. Understand State both definitions of a startup (the working one and RA 11337's legal one) and tell startups, small businesses, and SMEs apart.
  2. Apply Test any venture against the three marks: project origin, innovative solution, scalability.
  3. Analyze Take a venture's cost curve apart to tell scaling from growing.
  4. Evaluate Choose the ecosystem door (TBI, grant, accelerator, community) a founder can credibly walk through today.
