Mid-lesson explainer

Three SGF recipients. Three different doors.

The ecosystem map is in your head. Now watch three founders actually walk it.

Three real Startup Grant Fund recipients, three starting positions. One came through a university TBI with a thesis prototype and a faculty mentor. One came in cold, got rejected, rewrote the problem statement, and got in on the second try. One came through an accelerator with traction, using the grant for the critical feature no VC wanted to fund. Different paths, one shared move: each named a real problem in language a grant panel could parse without translation.

Watch for

  1. What each founder already had before applying: a mentor inside the network, a co-founder who could build, public traction. The door matched the assets, not the ambition.
  2. The second founder's rejection: what changed between attempt one and attempt two was the problem statement, not the idea.
  3. The closing rule, which is also your Field-6 rule: the right door is the one you can credibly walk through given what you have today.
