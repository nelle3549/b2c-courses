## In-content examples (not story blocks)

- **GCash and Smart Padala (§2 project origins):** GCash launched October 2004 by Globe subsidiary G-Xchange as an SMS-based money-transfer service; Smart's Smart Padala (2004) was the SMS-era remittance counterpart; both aimed at Filipinos without bank access; GCash reached its first 1M users in about two years. Sources: [Wikipedia/GCash history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GCash) · [SunStar 20-years feature](https://www.sunstar.com.ph/cebu/feature/gcash-20-years-of-transformative-financial-inclusion-in-the-philippines). Deck (Faculty Training 2026) uses the same pair.
- **Famous project origins (§2):** Google (Page & Brin's Stanford PhD research project), Apple (Jobs & Wozniak's hand-built computer, 1976), Facebook (Zuckerberg's student directory, 2004), Twitter (SMS side project, 2006). Same set as the Faculty Training deck, common knowledge, stated without contested superlatives (the deck's "world's first personal computer" claim is NOT repeated).
- **iPhone teardown margins (§3):** iPhone X parts ≈ $370.25 vs $999 retail (TechInsights teardown, 2017, widely reported); iPhone 16 Pro Max parts ≈ $485 (256GB) vs $1,199 retail, ~59.5% gross margin (TD Cowen/TechInsights estimates, 2024: [DigiTimes](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20241004PD214/apple-iphone-component-cost-production-profit.html) · [AndroidHeadlines](https://www.androidheadlines.com/2024/10/iphone-16-pro-maxs-bom-reveals-a-manufacturing-cost-of-485.html)). Deck's figures ($416 / $1,310.36) replaced with the sourced pair.
- **Top-10 market cap flip (§3):** 2011 global top 10 by market capitalization was dominated by oil, mining, and banks (2 tech companies); by 2021, 8 of 10 were technology platforms (PwC Global Top 100 rankings; deck cites Wikipedia's version of the same lists). Stated as "two of the ten" vs "eight of the ten."
- **RA 11337 (§1, §4):** exact definition from Section 3(g): "Startup refers to any person or registered entity in the Philippines which aims to develop an innovative product, process, or business model." Innovation defined to include product, process, AND business model (Sec. 3(c)-(e)). Benefits: registration/permit subsidies, facilities and office-space subsidies, GIA (Sec. 7); Startup Grant Fund under DOST, DICT, and DTI (Sec. 11); Startup Venture Fund under DTI+NDC (Sec. 12); startup visas (Sec. 13). Approved April 26, 2019. Source: [Lawphil full text](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2019/ra_11337_2019.html).
- **Working definition (§1):** "a temporary organization designed to search for a repeatable and scalable business model," attributed to Steve Blank (also the deck's generic definition).
