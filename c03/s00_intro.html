Mini-Course 03

Value Crafted: The Value Proposition Canvas

Course Intro · The trap that catches founders right after they validate.

You've validated a problem. The trap is what happens next: you leap straight to features, and six weeks later customers stare at you, polite but lost. This video introduces the canvas that slows that leap by 24 hours, long enough to map your solution to what the customer actually ranks first. Watch before Section 1.

You found the problem in Course 2. This course is about the translation step: turning a validated problem into an offer the customer recognizes the second they hear it. You'll work on the official Value Proposition Canvas, the same tool used by product teams worldwide, with the real moves of edamama, Angkas, Peddlr, and Kumu as your worked examples. As you go, you'll fill in your own Value Proposition Canvas, box by box: your customer's jobs, their top pains and gains ranked, your relievers and creators mapped one-to-one, what you ship, and a proposition written in your customer's own words. By the final page it's compiled in the canvas format, printable, and ready to be read aloud to a real customer.

By the end, you'll be able to turn a validated problem into a proposition your customer recognizes, without building features nobody ranked.

  1. Understand Explain why the canvas sits between problem validation and business-model design.
  2. Apply Rank customer pains and gains by frequency, severity, and addressability.
  3. Analyze Diagnose a misaligned canvas where the value side does not match the ranked pains and gains one-to-one.
  4. Evaluate Judge which value proposition best shows fit and customer-language wording.
