Mid-lesson explainer

Pains and gains are not opposites.

Same feature, two different propositions. The customer's job decides which one you're holding.

A customer says grocery shopping eats two hours every Saturday. Is "saves two hours" a pain reliever or a gain creator? The honest answer is: it depends on the job. This video walks the same feature through two different customer jobs and shows how the proposition, the message, and even the product change with the answer.

Watch for

  1. The rule: if you can't decide which side of the canvas an outcome belongs on, you don't know the customer's job well enough yet. Go back to your empathy notes.
  2. The two competing pitches for the same feature: "Stop wasting your weekend on groceries" versus "Spend Saturday morning with your kids, not in the aisle."
  3. Which of the two a customer is more likely to forward to a friend, and why that matters for your Field-5 proposition.
