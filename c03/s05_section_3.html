Your customer ranked three pains. You have the budget to build six features. What does the canvas say to do?

Three, mapped one to one. Extra features are not free value; they cost focus, money, and a describable product. If a feature does not point at a top-ranked pain or gain, the canvas says it floats, and floating features are how feature soup starts. The founders below built a hit by subtracting.
Field notes · real founders
Nel Laygo & Aiko Reyes · Peddlr · Catbalogan, Samar, 2021

Nel Laygo and Aiko Reyes built Peddlr for the stores of their hometown, shops they describe as stuck in time for more than 30 years, running on pen and paper. The app launched in July 2021 and the first week's data was rough: users were quitting after about seven days. So Laygo did the Course 2 move: he phoned them. The calls revealed one dominant job. People had downloaded a whole point-of-sale app because they wanted a single thing, inventory tracking. Many also feared typing their numbers into an internet app because of scams.

The response was subtraction, not addition. Peddlr went offline-first so it worked without signal and the data stayed on the owner's phone. The basic features stayed free. The core stayed at POS plus inventory, and the extras waited. Rest of World reported the result: 2.1 million downloads, around a million registered merchants, more than half of them sari-sari stores, and a US$4.3M seed round behind a team from Samar.

Every design choice answers a ranked pain: offline-first answers the signal and trust pains, free answers the cash pain, and the inventory core answers the job people actually came for. A value map with no floating features.
Sources: Rest of World (2022) · Fintech Global (2022) · BusinessWorld (2025)

The value map is the square on the left of the canvas, and it mirrors the circle. Pain relievers are the specific ways your offer removes a pain. Gain creators are the specific ways it produces a gain. Products and services are what you actually ship to deliver them. The discipline is one to one: for each of the top three pains, design one reliever; for each of the top three gains, one creator. Six items, six lines, nothing floating.

One more distinction before you map your own: a reliever and a creator can hide inside the same feature. Saving a customer two hours of grocery time is pain relief if their job is escaping a weekend chore, and a gain creator if their job is winning Saturday mornings with their kids. Same feature, opposite emotional posture, different pitch. The video on the next page makes that call concrete, and it is the single most common mistake founders make on this canvas.
